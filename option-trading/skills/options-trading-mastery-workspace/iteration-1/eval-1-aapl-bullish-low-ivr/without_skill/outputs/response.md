# AAPL Bullish Options Strategy - Low IVR Environment

## Market Context
- Current Stock Price: $195
- Time Horizon: ~6 weeks until earnings
- Implied Volatility Rank (IVR): 18 (Low)
- Your Outlook: Bullish rally expected

## Recommended Strategy: Bull Call Spread

Given the low IVR environment and your 6-week timeframe, a **Bull Call Spread** is the optimal choice.

### Why Bull Call Spread?
1. **Low IVR Advantage**: When IVR is low (18), you benefit from selling premium on the upper strike while buying the lower strike
2. **Limited Risk**: Maximum loss is predetermined (the width of the strikes minus credit received)
3. **Earnings Safety**: With defined risk, you can hold through earnings if the thesis holds
4. **Theta Decay**: Works in your favor on the short call
5. **Cost Efficiency**: Lower capital requirement than buying calls outright

## Specific Trade Setup

### Position Details
- **Strategy**: Bull Call Spread (Buy lower call / Sell higher call)
- **Buy Call**: $190 strike (In The Money - ITM)
- **Sell Call**: $200 strike (Out of The Money - OTM)
- **Expiration**: 6 weeks out (approximately April 18, 2026)
- **Quantity**: 1-2 spreads per $10,000 account capital

### Example Pricing (Estimated)
- Buy $190 Call: ~$8.50 debit
- Sell $200 Call: ~$3.50 credit
- **Net Cost**: $5.00 per spread ($500 per contract)
- **Max Profit**: $5.00 per spread ($500)
- **Max Loss**: $5.00 per spread ($500)
- **Risk/Reward**: 1:1

## Profit & Loss Management

### Take Profit Targets
1. **50% of Max Profit**: Exit when spread is worth $2.50 (close 50% of position)
   - Typically occurs when stock reaches $197-198
   - Provides quick win with reduced capital at risk

2. **75% of Max Profit**: Exit when spread is worth $3.75
   - Excellent risk/reward ratio achieved
   - Usually around $198-200 stock price

3. **Full Max Profit**: Let position run to expiration if stock closes above $200
   - Max profit realized at $200+ stock price
   - Hold if earnings thesis is still intact

### Stop Loss / Cut Loss Rules
1. **Hard Stop at 100% Loss**: If spread drops to $0.00 in value
   - Exit immediately regardless of thesis
   - Protects against unexpected downside

2. **Thesis Break Stop**: If AAPL closes below $190 with >3 weeks to expiration
   - Your bullish thesis has failed
   - Exit to preserve remaining capital

3. **Time Decay Stop**: If 3 weeks remain and stock hasn't moved above $197
   - Theta decay is now working against you
   - Exit and redeploy capital elsewhere

4. **Pre-Earnings Exit**: If earnings are 1 week away and stock hasn't rallied significantly
   - Reduce earnings gap risk by closing 50-75% of position
   - Take defined profit rather than risk earnings surprise

## Alternative Consideration: Long Call
If you're willing to risk more capital for unlimited upside:
- **Buy Call**: $195 strike (At The Money)
- **Expiration**: Same 6-week timeframe
- **Advantage**: Unlimited profit potential
- **Disadvantage**: Full premium at risk (~$4-6), higher cost
- **When to use**: If you're very confident in the rally

## Key Monitoring Points
- **Weekly**: Check if stock is progressing toward $200 target
- **News Catalyst**: Watch for positive earnings guidance or product announcements
- **Earnings Date**: Confirm exact date and adjust position accordingly
- **Greeks**: Monitor Delta (should be 0.5-0.7 for buy side) and Theta decay acceleration

## Summary
Use a **Bull Call Spread** with $190/$200 strikes expiring in 6 weeks. Take 50% profits at 50% max gain, exit at hard stops if thesis breaks, and manage around the earnings date. This strategy aligns with low IVR by selling the elevated short call premium while maintaining directional bullish exposure.
